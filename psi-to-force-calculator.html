Convert Pressure to Force

Use this PSI to force calculator to convert pressure and diameter into force using piston area. This is useful for pneumatic cylinders, hydraulic cylinders, presses, clamps, tooling, and general pressure-to-force calculations.

This calculator estimates theoretical force from pressure and area. Real-world output force can be lower due to friction, seal drag, pressure loss, rod area, and mechanical inefficiencies.

Force = Pressure × Area

Area = π × Diameter² / 4

What to check next

This tool is great for fast theoretical checks, but real machine force is usually lower than the raw math suggests. Pneumatic systems lose usable force through pressure drop, regulator performance, seal drag, rod-side area, misalignment, and overall mechanical inefficiency.

For actual application work, this page usually works best alongside a pneumatic force calculator, speed calculation, and air system sizing so you can confirm the machine has enough usable force at the actual operating conditions.
