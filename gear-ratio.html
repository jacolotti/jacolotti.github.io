How It Works

For each stage: stage ratio = driven teeth ÷ driver teeth

Total gear ratio is the product of all active stage ratios.

Output speed = input speed ÷ total ratio

Ideal output torque = input torque × total ratio

Estimated output torque = ideal torque × efficiency

Every external mesh reverses direction. Odd number of stages means final output rotates opposite the input. Even number of stages means final output rotates the same direction as the input.

About This Gear Ratio Calculator

This free gear ratio calculator helps with quick engineering estimates for simple and compound external gear trains. It can be used for machine design, conveyors, power transmission, automation equipment, servo-driven mechanisms, and prototype development.

In a basic gear pair, the ratio is found by dividing the number of teeth on the driven gear by the number of teeth on the driver gear. A larger driven gear causes speed reduction and torque increase. A smaller driven gear creates an overdrive condition and increases output speed while reducing torque.

For multi-stage gear trains, the total ratio is the product of all individual stage ratios. This allows large overall reductions to be achieved in a compact mechanical system.

This calculator is intended for estimation and design comparison. Real-world performance can vary due to efficiency losses, bearing drag, backlash, tooth profile effects, lubrication, mounting errors, and actual system loading.
