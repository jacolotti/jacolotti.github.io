What L10 life means

L10 life is the basic rating life at which 90% of a sufficiently large group of identical bearings are expected to still be operating under the stated conditions. It is a comparison and selection tool, not a guarantee of actual field life.

Real bearing life can be heavily affected by lubrication, contamination, misalignment, shock loading, mounting quality, preload, temperature, and duty cycle. That means two applications with the same calculated L10 value can perform very differently in the real world.

Why load matters so much

Bearing life drops fast as applied load rises. Even a modest increase in equivalent load can cause a major reduction in calculated life, which makes proper sizing and load path review extremely important.

Common real-world life reducers

  • Poor lubrication
  • Contamination and debris
  • Misalignment
  • Shock loading
  • Improper mounting or preload
  • Heat and duty cycle effects
