Continue your pneumatic design

Air consumption is only one part of a good pneumatic design. After estimating demand, you usually still need to verify cylinder force, expected actuator speed, valve sizing, tubing pressure loss, and plant air capacity.

This is especially important when cylinders run fast, cycle often, or several actuators fire at the same time on the same machine.

Quick design reminders

Real usage is often higher than textbook volume because of:

  • Leakage and fittings
  • Dead volume in tubing and valves
  • Cushioning and timing losses
  • Pressure drop under high demand
  • Multiple actuators cycling together
