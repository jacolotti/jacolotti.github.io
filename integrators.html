Choosing the Right Category

What Type of Help Do You Actually Need?

A common mistake is treating every automation problem like it needs the same kind of provider. A robot cell, a servo axis issue, a welded frame, a panel build, and a pneumatic clamp problem may all be part of automation, but they usually need different skills.

Use General Automation Integrators When...

You need PLC programming, machine integration, troubleshooting, equipment upgrades, or a partner that can coordinate multiple parts of a system.

Use Motion & Servo Specialists When...

The project depends on positioning, indexing, servo tuning, torque, acceleration, gearing, coordinated motion, or repeatable axis performance.

Use Pneumatic & Tooling Specialists When...

The problem involves cylinders, clamps, grippers, valve manifolds, air consumption, cycle speed, pneumatic force, or machine tooling.

Use Controls & Panel Shops When...

You need panels built, electrical cabinets updated, field wiring support, schematics, controls hardware layout, or production electrical support.

Use Machine Design / Builders When...

You need a frame, mechanism, fixture, guarding concept, custom station, machine structure, or mechanical design support before controls work begins.

Use Fabrication & Machine Shops When...

You need machined parts, weldments, brackets, fixtures, tooling plates, structural work, or physical build support for automation equipment.

Request Quality

What to Include Before You Submit a Project Request

A good request gets a better response. You do not need a finished design, but you should describe the process clearly enough that a provider can understand the type of work, risk, and timing.

Process or Machine Type Describe what the equipment does, what product it handles, and where the problem occurs.
Current Situation Explain whether this is a new build, retrofit, production issue, repair, upgrade, or early planning request.
Controls Platform Include PLC, HMI, robot, servo, vision, safety, or communication platforms if you know them.
Mechanical Scope Mention frames, tooling, fixtures, guarding, cylinders, motors, conveyors, weldments, or machining needs.
Timing State whether this is urgent downtime, a planned upgrade, a budget quote, or a future project.
Region Include whether you need local onsite support, remote help, or a partner who can travel.
