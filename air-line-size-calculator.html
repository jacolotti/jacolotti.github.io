Compressed Air Line Size Calculator

Estimate recommended compressed air pipe size using SCFM, line length, operating pressure, and a target air velocity. This is a practical starting-point tool for pneumatic system design, machine drops, valve supply checks, and early automation layout work.

In general, undersized air lines cause pressure loss, poor cylinder performance, slow actuators, unstable tooling, and wasted debug time. This calculator helps you choose a better starting line size before final design review.

Enter values and press Calculate.

This calculator uses a practical sizing method based on air velocity and a simple line-length penalty. It is intended for early design and troubleshooting. Final sizing should also consider fittings, quick disconnects, regulators, filters, hose restrictions, branch drops, simultaneous demand, and allowable pressure drop at peak flow.

Typical Components That Affect Real Air Performance

Real-world pneumatic performance is not just about pipe diameter. Regulators, filter assemblies, push-to-connect fittings, solenoid valves, manifolds, and flexible hose sections can all choke flow long before the main pipe does.

This makes good line sizing even more important on machines with fast cylinders, vacuum generation, blow-off circuits, or multiple devices firing at the same time.

How to Use This Air Line Size Calculator

Enter your expected airflow in SCFM, the equivalent line length, and the operating pressure. Then choose how conservative you want to be on air velocity. Lower velocity targets usually mean larger pipe, lower pressure loss, and better pneumatic response.

For short machine drops with modest flow, a practical velocity target may be enough. For longer runs, future machine expansion, or sensitive tooling, choose a more conservative target.
