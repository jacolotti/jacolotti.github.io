Before blaming the PLC

  • Verify device power is actually present under load, not just with no current draw.
  • Check whether the issue is constant or intermittent.
  • Compare what the device is doing physically versus what the PLC tag says.
  • Look for changes: new hardware, new firmware, rewiring, moved cables, or changed IP settings.
  • Confirm whether one device is failing or an entire segment/network is affected.

Typical root causes

  • Wrong IP address, subnet, or duplicate addressing
  • Device powered but not actually communicating
  • Loose or damaged Ethernet cable / connector
  • Managed switch setting mismatch or bad port
  • Scan time too slow for the signal duration
  • Incorrect analog scaling or engineering unit conversion
  • Voltage drop, weak 24V supply, or grounding noise
