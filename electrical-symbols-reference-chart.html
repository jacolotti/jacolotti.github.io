Industrial Electrical Symbols Reference Chart

This industrial electrical symbols reference chart gives you a quick way to review common schematic and control panel symbols used in automation, PLC systems, relay logic, motor circuits, and safety devices.

If you work with ladder diagrams, panel prints, machine wiring, or troubleshooting documentation, knowing these symbols saves time and reduces mistakes when tracing circuits or reviewing drawings.

Important: Symbol styles can vary slightly between companies, print packages, CAD software, and standards. Always verify against your plant standards, customer requirements, and the actual drawing legend when available.

Power and Ground Symbols

Ground

Ground

Used to show a connection to earth ground or system common ground depending on the drawing style.

DC Power

DC Supply

Common on 24VDC control circuits, sensor power, PLC I/O power, and instrumentation loops.

AC Power

AC Supply

Used to show an AC power source feeding a panel, transformer, motor circuit, or branch circuit.

Protection and Switching Symbols

Fuse

Fuse

Protects a circuit from overcurrent. Common in control transformers, PLC power feeds, and branch circuits.

Circuit Breaker

Circuit Breaker

Used for overcurrent protection and isolation. Often found on incoming power or branch loads.

Disconnect Switch

Disconnect Switch

Used to isolate equipment for service or lockout. Common at machine main disconnects.

Relay and Contactor Symbols

Relay / Contactor Coil

Relay or Contactor Coil

Represents the energized coil that changes contact states elsewhere in the circuit.

Normally Open Contact

Normally Open (NO) Contact

Open in the normal state and closes when actuated or when the associated coil energizes.

Normally Closed Contact

Normally Closed (NC) Contact

Closed in the normal state and opens when actuated or when the associated coil energizes.

Motor and Transformer Symbols

Motor

Motor

Represents a motor load. In industrial prints this may refer to a conveyor motor, pump motor, fan motor, or servo-related drive output.

Transformer

Transformer

Used to step voltage up or down, often from higher machine voltage to 120VAC or 24VAC control circuits.

Overload Relay

Overload Relay

Provides motor overload protection and often includes auxiliary contacts for fault indication or control logic interruption.

Field Devices and Operator Controls

Pushbutton

Pushbutton

Represents an operator pushbutton such as start, stop, reset, or cycle start.

Pilot Light

Pilot Light

Used to show visual indication devices such as power on, machine run, fault, or safety circuit status.

Proximity Sensor

Proximity Sensor

Represents a non-contact sensor used for part detection, position sensing, or machine interlocks.

PLC and Control Wiring Symbols

Terminal Block

Terminal Block

Used to show field wire landing points and organized wire transitions inside a panel.

PLC Input

PLC Input

Represents a discrete PLC input point receiving a signal from a switch, sensor, or device contact.

PLC Output

PLC Output

Represents a discrete PLC output driving a relay coil, solenoid, contactor, pilot light, or other load.

Safety Circuit Symbols

Emergency Stop

E-Stop

Represents an emergency stop device used to remove hazardous motion or force when activated.

Safety Relay

Safety Relay

Used in safety circuits to monitor devices like E-stops, gate switches, and dual-channel inputs.

Guard Switch

Guard Door Switch

Represents a monitored guard interlock or safety switch used on gates, doors, or access panels.

How to Use This Chart

  • Use it as a quick reference while reading control panel schematics or troubleshooting ladder logic wiring.
  • Compare symbols with your plant’s print legend because standards and drafting styles can vary.
  • Follow the circuit function, not just the symbol shape. Wire numbers, device tags, and surrounding logic matter.
  • When troubleshooting, trace power first, then control voltage, then device state changes.
